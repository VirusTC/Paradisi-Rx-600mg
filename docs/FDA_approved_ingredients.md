To maintain absolute scientific accuracy under the standards of pharmacognosy and international drug compendia, it is critical to address a core rule of botanical chemistry: USP-grade pharmaceutical citric acid is a chemically isolated, pure substance (C₆H₈O₇). Because it is a single, pure molecule, its underlying clinical mechanisms do not change based on the source plant. [1, 2]

Furthermore, from a botanical standpoint, there are no different "species" of Fructus Citri Paradisi---all Fructus Citri Paradisi varieties belong to the exact same single hybrid species, *Citrus × paradisi*. In pharmaceutical manufacturing and traditional pharmacognosy, the universal pharmaceutical drug name assigned to the plant material across all varieties is *Fructus Citri Paradisi*. [3, 4]

However, when using our proprietary freeze-dry or centrifugal extraction protocols, the citric acid is not isolated as a naked chemical sheet. Instead, it is extracted as a native clinical matrix. Because our method explicitly avoids chemical solvents, the final USP-grade material retains the distinctive, co-extracted bio-synergistic compounds unique to each *cultivar class* (White, Pink, and Red). These target compounds alter the compound's final clinical behaviors. [5]

Technical Verification of our Process
--------------------------------------

Because our mechanical extraction relies purely on lyophilization (sublimation under vacuum) and ultra-centrifugation (>9,000 × g), these complex organic matrices remain completely intact. Traditional chemical extraction uses harsh sulfuric acid to force precipitation, which cleaves away these sensitive companion compounds and leaves behind chemical impurities. our process ensures the final pharmaceutical powder retains the natural, targeted health benefits of the original fruit.
